Bandeja Paisa

Bandeja Paisa is a traditional Colombian dish that originates from the Antioquia region, known for its hearty and flavorful combination of proteins, beans, rice, and plantains. It’s a generous platter that showcases the diverse tastes of Colombian cuisine. The dish typically includes a variety of meats such as beef, pork, and sausage, as well as beans, rice, fried plantains, and a fried egg. It’s often served in large portions and is a true reflection of Colombia’s rich agricultural and culinary heritage.

Ingredients for 4px

for the meat

  • 300g (10 oz) ground beef (for carne molida or ground beef stew)
  • 4 chorizo sausages (Colombian-style, or any mild pork sausage)
  • 4 slices of pork belly or 200g (7 oz) pork ribs
  • 4 small pieces of beef steak (about 150g or 5 oz each)
  • 4 fried eggs (one for each serving)

For the sides

  • 2 cups of red beans (or black beans, cooked and seasoned)
  • 2 cups of white rice (cooked)
  • 4 small fried plantains (tostones or maduros, depending on preference)
  • 1 avocado (sliced)
  • 1 lime (cut into wedges)
  • 1 small bunch of fresh cilantro (optional, for garnish)

Process

  1. Cook the Beans:
    • If you're using dried beans, soak them overnight and then cook in a large pot with water, salt, and a couple of garlic cloves until soft (about 1-1.5 hours). Alternatively, canned beans can be used—just heat them up with some onion, garlic, and a bit of salt to enhance their flavor.
  2. Cook the Rice:
    • Cook the rice according to your preferred method (typically 1 part rice to 2 parts water), adding a pinch of salt. Once cooked, keep it warm for serving.
  3. Cook the Meats:
    • Beef Steak: Season the beef steaks with salt, pepper, and garlic powder. Heat a bit of oil in a skillet over medium-high heat and cook the steaks to your desired level of doneness (about 4-5 minutes per side for medium).
    • Ground Beef: In a separate pan, cook the ground beef with a little oil over medium heat. Season with salt, pepper, and garlic, and cook until browned and fully cooked (about 8-10 minutes).
    • Pork Belly/Ribs: Fry the pork belly in a hot pan with a bit of oil until crispy and golden. Alternatively, if using pork ribs, grill or pan-fry them for about 6-8 minutes on each side until they’re well-cooked and crispy on the outside.
    • Chorizo: Grill or pan-fry the chorizo sausages until browned and cooked through (about 10 minutes). Cut into slices for easy serving.
  4. Prepare the Fried Plantains:
    • For tostones (savory fried plantains), peel and slice green plantains into thick rounds. Fry them in hot oil until golden, then remove and flatten them using a flat tool or the bottom of a cup. Return them to the oil and fry again until crispy and golden. Drain on paper towels and sprinkle with a pinch of salt.
    • For maduros (sweet fried plantains), peel and slice ripe plantains diagonally. Fry in hot oil until golden brown and caramelized (about 2-3 minutes per side).
  5. Fry the Eggs:
    • In a separate skillet, fry one egg per person to your preferred level of doneness (typically sunny side up or over-easy).
  6. Assemble the Bandeja Paisa:
    • On each large plate, begin by adding a generous portion of white rice and a scoop of beans.
    • Arrange the cooked meats: place a piece of steak, a spoonful of ground beef, a slice of chorizo, and some crispy pork belly or ribs on the plate.
    • Add the fried plantains (either tostones or maduros, depending on your preference).
    • Place a fried egg on top of the rice or beside the meats.
    • Garnish with slices of avocado, a few lime wedges for squeezing, and a sprinkle of fresh cilantro (optional).
